About Patsy Mink

When Was She Born?

Patsy Mink was born December 6, 1927 (in Hawaii), and Died September 28, 2002

What firsts did she set?

  • Patsy Mink was the first woman of color and the first Asian American woman in Congress.
  • Patsy Mink was also the first Asian-American to run for U.S. President.

What's her story and what did she do?

  • Mink decided to tackle gender inequality by becoming a politician, after a series of medical school rejections led Mink to run for Congress--and create better educational opportunities for other women.
  • Lastly Mink Helped Write Title IX.

Her Story

Patsy Mink was devastated after being declined into multiple medical schools, despite good grades, soley based on gender. After the "medical incident" Mink decided to try and become a lawyer. Even after passing the bar exam, Patsy Mink wasn't allowed to be a lawyer Mink was unable to find a job because of her interracial marriage. She decided to start her own practice instead and founded the Oahu Young Democrats in 1954. Eventually, Patsy Mink got a job in congress and was hard at work, trying to get equal opportunities for women in sports and education. Patsy Mink finally got what she was dreaming of with Title IX.

Title IX of the Education Amendments Act of 1972 is a federal law that states: "No person in the United States shall, on the basis of sex, be excluded from participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination under any education program or activity receiving Federal financial assistance."
